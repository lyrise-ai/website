# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Employers**: CTOs, VPs of Engineering, and hiring managers at growth-stage companies and enterprises who need to hire AI/ML, Data Science, or Software Engineers quickly and confidently. They arrive with urgency and skepticism, having been burned by traditional recruiters or offshore outsourcing. They want to be proven wrong.

**Candidates**: AI/ML engineers, data scientists, and software engineers seeking vetted, high-quality opportunities. They want to feel respected, not processed.

Both audiences share this site. The brand must feel equally credible to each without splitting into two identities.

## Product Purpose

LyRise is an AI-powered tech talent hiring platform that matches employers with pre-vetted AI/ML engineers. It also offers an AI accelerator program for candidates. The platform positions itself as the expert intermediary: faster than traditional staffing, more credible than generic job boards, more human than offshore churn-and-burn vendors.

Success looks like: an employer books a meeting or submits a hire request within their first visit. A candidate trusts the platform enough to apply.

## Brand Personality

Expert, efficient, trustworthy.

Voice is direct and confident, not salesy. LyRise knows what it does well and states it plainly. No hedge words, no superlatives, no filler.

The emotional goal for every employer: **clarity and control**. "Finally, a hiring process I can understand and trust." Not excitement, not urgency. Settled confidence.

## References

**Linear / Vercel**: Product-company brand aesthetic. Minimal, fast, high-craft. The interface feels considered. Nothing wasted, nothing missing. Typography carries weight. Whitespace is intentional, not empty.

This is the bar: marketing that feels like it was built by people who also built excellent software.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS (Intercom-blue)**: Gradient blobs, stock photography of diverse hands on a laptop, "AI-powered" stamped on everything, MUI defaults left unstyled. This is the baseline to exceed, not match.
- **Staffing agency stiff**: Traditional recruitment firm aesthetics. Clip-art icons, corporate stock photos, dense tables of "our process." Feels like a 2012 HR vendor. Institutional in the wrong way.
- **Hacker / terminal dark**: Dark-mode-first, code-font heavy, green-on-black energy. This speaks to engineers but alienates the buyers (employers). LyRise serves both.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity is the trust signal.** Don't prove expertise through visual complexity or feature density. A clear, navigable interface IS the credibility claim. Employers who can't understand the page in 10 seconds will leave.

2. **Show outcomes, not process.** Employers care that they'll hire the right person in two weeks, not how LyRise's seven-step vetting methodology works. Lead with results, outcomes, and proof. Save the methodology for those who dig.

3. **Precision over decoration.** Every visual element must communicate something. No background shapes, gradient orbs, or decorative illustrations that don't carry meaning. The restraint is the statement.

4. **One brand, two audiences.** Employer and candidate flows must feel like the same product family. No jarring register shift. The authority that attracts employers should also reassure candidates they're joining something serious.

5. **Product-grade craft on a brand surface.** The reference is Linear and Vercel, not a corporate marketing agency deck. Every component, every spacing decision, every micro-interaction should feel as considered as the best SaaS product UIs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: WCAG AA (4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components). Keyboard navigable. Screen reader compatible with semantic HTML and ARIA labels where needed. Support `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations.
